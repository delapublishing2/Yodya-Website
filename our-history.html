  • First Black Republic: Haiti is the first independent nation in Latin America and the Caribbean, and the first republic to be led by people of African descent after a successful slave rebellion against French colonial rule in the late 18th century.
  • Cuisine: Haitian cuisine is a delightful blend of African, French, and indigenous Taíno influences. Dishes like Griot (fried pork) and Tasso de Dinde (spiced turkey) are popular, often accompanied by rice and beans.
  • Artistic Heritage: Haiti has a vibrant arts scene, particularly renowned for its colorful paintings, sculptures, and handicrafts. The art often reflects the country’s history, spirituality, and everyday life.
  • Music and Dance: Haitian music is diverse, ranging from traditional folk songs to popular styles like compas and rara. The dances, such as the energetic kompa dance, are integral to Haitian cultural celebrations.
  • Citadelle Laferrière: This impressive mountaintop fortress, built in the early 19th century to defend against potential French invasion, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of Haiti's independence.

1920
East Coast bound...

UPS shifted its operations to New York City, marking the start of an era of expansion that would eventually take the company to the East Coast and beyond.

1930
No stopping this service...

Throughout the 1930s and 1940s, UPS continued to grow, pioneering innovative delivery methods and expanding its reach. The company introduced the concept of consolidated delivery, where packages from multiple senders are delivered to a single destination, revolutionizing the industry.

1975
The Golden Link...

In 1975, UPS became the first package delivery company to serve every address in the 48 contiguous United States. This milestone marked the beginning of the company's global expansion efforts.

1985
Next day? No problem...

In 1985, UPS launched its next-day air delivery service, making it possible to reach every address in the United States within 24 hours. This service became the cornerstone of UPS's express delivery capabilities.

1988
An airline is born...

In 1988, UPS received authorization from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) to operate its own airline, UPS Airlines. This development allowed UPS to have greater control over its air delivery operations and expand its international reach.

1991
A green new home...

In 1991, UPS moved its corporate headquarters to Atlanta, Georgia, and undertook a comprehensive sustainability initiative to reduce its environmental footprint. This included investing in alternative fuel vehicles and optimizing delivery routes to reduce emissions.

1994
A bold financial step...

In 1999, UPS went public, offering shares of the company on the New York Stock Exchange. This move provided UPS with the capital needed to further expand its global operations and invest in new technologies.

2001
Diving into retail...

In 2001, UPS acquired Mail Boxes Etc., rebranding the stores as The UPS Store. This expansion into retail allowed UPS to offer a wide range of services, from shipping and printing to mailbox rentals, at convenient locations across the country.

2013
Who doesn’t love the weekend?...

In 2013, UPS introduced Saturday delivery, meeting increasing demand for faster delivery times. This service enhancement provided greater convenience to customers and improved the company's competitive edge.

2019
A new direction...

In 2019, UPS began its transformation to UPS Digital and Innovation, harnessing the power of technology to improve efficiency, enhance customer experience, and drive sustainable growth.
