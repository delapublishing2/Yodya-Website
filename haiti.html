About Haiti

Did you know Haiti is the first independent nation in Latin America and the Caribbean, having won its freedom from French colonial rule in 1804? That's just the start. Haiti boasts stunning landscapes, from its lush mountains to pristine beaches that rival any postcard. Its vibrant culture dances to the beats of Compas music and celebrates rich traditions like Carnival, known worldwide for its colorful parades and intricate masks. But wait, there's more! Haiti is home to the Citadelle Laferrière, a majestic fortress perched high in the mountains, and the enchanting Bassin Bleu waterfalls, where turquoise waters cascade into natural pools. Whether you're a history buff, nature lover, or simply seeking adventure, Haiti promises an unforgettable experience.

The Current Crisis

The situation is dire, with multiple crises hitting hard. Check out these key issues:

  • Escalating Gang Violence: Since February 2024, gang violence has surged, displacing over 15,000 people in just one week and tightening its grip on Port-au-Prince. Reports suggest gangs control about 80% of the city, even the airport, making it tough to get aid to those who need it most. (Source: World Vision)
  • Humanitarian Crisis: This violence is sparking a massive humanitarian catastrophe. Nearly 5.5 million Haitians, including 3 million children, urgently need help. There are severe shortages of food, limited access to healthcare, and a cholera outbreak that's only getting worse. (Source: World Vision)
  • Food Insecurity: The violence is messing up food deliveries and causing shortages. Millions are going hungry, with almost half of the population (around 5 million people) needing food assistance. (Source: Concern Worldwide)
  • Healthcare Collapse: The healthcare system is crumbling under the pressure. Many hospitals can't operate due to attacks and lack of supplies. On top of that, there's a cholera outbreak with over 82,000 cases reported. (Sources: UNICEF, Concern Worldwide)
  • Political Instability: Haiti hasn't had elected leaders since 2016, and the president's assassination in 2021 made things even worse. This vacuum in leadership has given gangs more room to thrive. (Source: GIS Reports)

    • The situation in Haiti is heart-wrenching, and the numbers tell a tough story. International aid is crucial right now, but we also need long-term solutions to tackle the root causes of these crises.

Our Mission

FONYO is a coordination that brings together the aspirations of other foundations within the Haitian community. It comprises members (founders, honorary members, and supporters) who are courageous, motivated, knowledgeable, duty-bound, and responsible young people committed to saying no to violence in all its forms and layers and yes to equal rights.
