About FONYO

FONYO is a foundation of young scientists and intellectuals in the country, initiated in the West Department: Carrefour, Petit Goâve, and the Artibonite Department: Gonaïves and Saint Michel de l'Attalaye on October 24, 2008, marking its official public utility launch.

À Propos De/About Fonyo

Recognized for its socio-cultural, professional, economic, and other actions within the community, it is perceived as one of the foundations striving to reclaim the intellectual ideal and contribute to social life and public affairs. It is a socio-professional, scientific, cultural, and other types of foundation, neutral from public authorities and non-profit.

Objectives of FONYO

Professional Knowledge

Help young people enhance their professional knowledge for the benefit of society.

Civic Education

Aim to engage our young intellectuals in civic education for illiterate people.

Promote Action Programs

Promote action programs to strengthen the capacity of our agrarian system, environment, infrastructure, sanitation, health, reforestation, and others.

Encourage Commitment

Encourage young people to fully commit to the structural evolution of scientific affairs and all other areas.

Help Us Assist Haiti

Haiti is in crisis, and we need your hand to help us assist the country. Let us band together and support Haiti

  • First Black Republic: Haiti is the first independent nation in Latin America and the Caribbean, and the first republic to be led by people of African descent after a successful slave rebellion against French colonial rule in the late 18th century.
  • Cuisine: Haitian cuisine is a delightful blend of African, French, and indigenous Taíno influences. Dishes like Griot (fried pork) and Tasso de Dinde (spiced turkey) are popular, often accompanied by rice and beans.
  • Artistic Heritage: Haiti has a vibrant arts scene, particularly renowned for its colorful paintings, sculptures, and handicrafts. The art often reflects the country’s history, spirituality, and everyday life.
  • Music and Dance: Haitian music is diverse, ranging from traditional folk songs to popular styles like compas and rara. The dances, such as the energetic kompa dance, are integral to Haitian cultural celebrations.
  • Citadelle Laferrière: This impressive mountaintop fortress, built in the early 19th century to defend against potential French invasion, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of Haiti's independence.
